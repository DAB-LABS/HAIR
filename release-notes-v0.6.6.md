v0.6.6 (Mirror) is the release where HAIR starts telling the truth in both directions: the Sniffer shows everything it hears, and the new Mirror tab shows everything your Home Assistant transmits. Numbered 6.6 because a mirror should read the same both ways; 0.6.2 through 0.6.5 do not exist.

**The Mirror tab.** Every IR command sent through Home Assistant now lands as a row on a new panel tab, whether anything heard it or not: HAIR device commands, catalog tests, automations, and other integrations sending through the native infrared platform. Each row shows what was sent, which emitter carried it, whether a receiver heard it back and in which room, where it came from, and a running send count. A send that arrives while you are watching blooms the row silver. A command that transmits but is never heard reads "not heard" in quiet grey, which is how you find a dead IR LED or a misaimed blaster without pointing a phone camera at anything; the amber Not heard filter is there when you are actually troubleshooting, and transmit-only homes are treated as normal, not alarming.

**A third road for importing codes.** Mirror rows carry the same Assign, Test, and Trigger buttons as the rest of the panel. Press a button in any vendor app whose blaster transmits through the infrared platform, and if a receiver hears it, the decoded code appears on the Mirror one Assign away from living in HAIR. No pasting, no vendor support file, no re-learning. The Clipper pastes, the Plucker pulls, the Mirror overhears.

**Assigned signals stay visible.** Since v0.4.0, once you assigned a signal to a device command, re-pressing that button on the physical remote was silently swallowed, which made the Sniffer look broken exactly when everything was working. That suppression existed to keep HAIR's own transmissions out of the feed, and the Mirror's echo attribution now does that properly, so the suppression is gone. An assigned button flashes in the Sniffer forever, a deleted signal row comes back the next time the button is pressed, and dismissing the remote remains the one way to hide activity. If buttons you assigned long ago seem to reappear in your Sniffer after this update, that is not a bug: those are real presses HAIR was hiding from you.

**Triggers never fire on the house's own sends.** When Home Assistant transmits a command and a receiver hears the echo, that capture is attributed to the send instead of being treated as a new signal. A trigger now means "this arrived from the outside world," a physical remote or another app, and can never feed back on HAIR's own output.

**The assigned popover.** Clicking Assign on a signal that already has assignments opens a picker: add another assignment, or click an existing one to jump straight to that device's card, the same flow the Trigger button has had since v0.5.7.

**The v0.6.1 known issue is retired.** A trigger no longer loses its yellow badge when the startup heal merges duplicate rows; with assigned signals staying visible, the next press recreates the row and the badge re-attaches through the decoded identity.

Also in this release: the min-hits window on triggers now anchors at the first press, so "4 presses within 5 seconds" means exactly that; the assign dialog pre-fills Send times from the signal alongside Ditto count; count dots always show their number, including one; emitter reconnect blips no longer produce phantom send entries; and the row action buttons share one style module across every tab so the chips can never drift apart again.
