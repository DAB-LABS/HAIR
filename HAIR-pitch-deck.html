The Problem

Home Assistant has a tangled, knotted IR situation.

For years, IR control in HA has been the smart home equivalent of bedhead — functional, but nobody wants to be seen with it.

The current IR experience

  • SmartIR requires hand-editing JSON files
  • Broadlink learning is a 7-step Developer Tools dance
  • ESPHome IR demands YAML + firmware flashes
  • SwitchBot/Tuya force you to a cloud you don't trust
  • Logitech Harmony? Discontinued.

The result

120K+ HA users with IR-capable hardware.

Most never finish the setup.

The ones who do, never want to do it again.

It's a bad hair day. Every day.

The Solution

Meet HAIR. Home Assistant IR, restyled.

A third-party HA integration that makes capturing and managing IR commands feel magical, not technical.

What we cut out

  • YAML files
  • JSON device codes
  • Service-call gymnastics
  • Cloud dependencies
  • Hardware lock-in

What we style in

  • 2-click capture flow with instant test
  • Guided checklist per device type
  • Auto-mapping to HA entities
  • Works with any IR proxy
  • 100% local. 100% UI-driven.

Setup time: 7 steps → under 60 seconds

08
The Product

The capture flow is the cut.

Everything else is conditioner. The capture experience is the styling that makes users stay.

Old way (e.g., Broadlink):

  1. Open Developer Tools
  2. Call broadlink.learn service
  3. Point remote at Broadlink
  4. Press button
  5. Wait for service call return
  6. Copy raw data
  7. Paste into YAML or SmartIR JSON

7 steps. 3 require dev knowledge. 0 are fun.

HAIR way:

  1. Click "Learn" on the suggested command
  2. Press the button on your remote
  3. Click "Save & Learn Next"

3 steps. 0 require dev knowledge. 100% delight.
